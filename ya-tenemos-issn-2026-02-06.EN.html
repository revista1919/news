We Now Have an ISSN!

Editorial Staff • 02/05/2026

Dear community:

We are pleased to announce that our journal now has an ISSN. The National Agency for Research and Development of Chile (ANID), the body responsible for assigning this identifier in the country, has officially granted us this registration, representing a significant advancement and positioning our journal at a professional standard within the academic field.

What is ISSN?

The ISSN (International Standard Serial Number) is an international code assigned to serial publications, such as scientific journals, with the purpose of uniquely identifying them worldwide. Having an ISSN facilitates recognition, enhances visibility, and enables the journal to be indexable on multiple academic platforms, as is the case with professional journals like Science or Nature.

What does this mean for the journal?

This achievement will allow our journal to have greater reach and impact, to be officially cited, and to open new opportunities for accessing indexing systems that strengthen the dissemination of academic and scientific work developed by students.

This is just one more step in our growth. There is still much to improve and build, but it is undoubtedly a major milestone for The National Review of Sciences for Students.

Additionally, we inform that, according to ANID regulations, the official abbreviation of the journal becomes: Rev. nac. cienc. estud.

We thank the entire community for their constant support and for being part of this project.
